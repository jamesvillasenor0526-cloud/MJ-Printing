Print-Ready File Guide

Follow these simple guidelines to ensure your prints come out looking crisp, clear, and exactly as you imagined.

1

Resolution Matters via DPI

Most images found on the internet are 72 DPI (Dots Per Inch), which looks great on screens but blurry when printed.

For Printing: Always use 300 DPI or higher at actual size.

Checking your file: Right-click image > Properties > Details tab.

2

CMYK over RGB

Screens use RGB (Red, Green, Blue) light to display color. Printers use CMYK (Cyan, Magenta, Yellow, Key/Black) ink.

If you submit an RGB file, colors may shift (usually becoming duller) when converted to CMYK for printing.

Design in CMYK mode to see accurate colors before printing.

3

Full Bleed & Safety Zone

To avoid white borders, extend your background artwork 0.125 inches (3mm) beyond the trim edge.

  • Trim Line: Where the cutter slices the paper.
  • Bleed Area: Artwork extends here (gets cut off).
  • Safety Zone: Keep text/logos 0.125" inside the trim line.
